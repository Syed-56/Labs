AUTOMATING SCRIPT
You can schedule your scripts to run at any time. Note, in automation always use full
absolute paths of scripts.
at
● Use this command if you want your script to run at a time exactly one. Eg: To run
your script at 2 am. We will do
at 14:00
at> ./script.sh
at> <press ctrl+D to save>
● There are different time formats like 14:00, at 10:30pm, at now +1 minute, at
tomorrow.
To show list of all scheduled tasks, use atq
To remove a scheduled task, use atrm [job_number]
Crontab
It is for constant scheduling like if you want to run a script every day/hour/week.

When you type crontab -e for editing, you will a format like * * * * *
/path/scrpit.sh
First Star is for minute (0-59)
2nd Star is for hour (0-23)
3rd Star is for day of month (0-31)
4th Star is for month (0-12)
5th Star is for day of week (0-6) where 0 is
sunday
Everyday at Midnight - 0 0 * * * /path/script.sh
Every Monday at 8:30 AM - 30 8 * * 1 /path/script.sh
Every 15 minutes - */15 * * * * /path/script.sh
To check existing jobs (already scheduled ones), use crontab -l and to add a new job,
use crontab -e.
